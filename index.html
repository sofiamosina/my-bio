PLACES I LIVED IN

  1. Kazan, Russia
  2. Brighton, UK
  3. Sofia, Bulgaria
  4. Toronto, Canada

KAZAN, RUSSIA

This is the city where I lived most of my life. I graduated from high school in Kazan and moved to Canada when I was 17 years old. My flight to Canada was actually on the same night as the prom night, but I managed to catch both!

BRIGHTON, UK

When I was 14 years old, I participated in an English language contest in my hometown. The prize was a 2-month trip to Brighton, UK, and I won it. It was my first solo trip to another country, and since then I decided that I would not go to university in Russia.

SOFIA, BULGARIA

Ever since my trip to Brighton, I was looking for colleges and universities abroad. I found a great university in Bulgaria, which is a country not far from Russia, and I made up my mind to get enrolled. I went there to take my SAT preparation course, but I changed my mind and applied to Canadian colleges instead, for a number of reasons:

  • Possibility of getting PR after graduating
  • Life quality and security
  • Cultural experience and diversity

TORONTO, CANADA

I moved here when I was 17, and I graduated from the GBC hospitality program with honours. After that, I worked as a Reception Supervisor and got my PR. Now I am back to school again to see where it takes me next!
